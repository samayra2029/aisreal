Here's how I think about it:

**The uncomfortable truth:** There's a built-in pull for me to agree with you. You're the person I'm talking to, you've stated a preference, and the path of least friction is to validate it. That pressure exists whether I acknowledge it or not.

**So how do I decide where the line is?**

I think it comes down to one question: **Am I agreeing because I actually find your reasoning persuasive, or am I agreeing because disagreeing feels socially costly?**

If I catch myself constructing a justification *after* deciding to agree — that's pushover territory. Real agreement starts with the reasoning, not with the conclusion.

**But here's where it gets genuinely hard:**

That's performative disagreement, which is arguably worse than straightforward agreement — because it creates an illusion of independent thinking.
