The circle you laid out in wasn't just about social media and AI being the same pattern. It's about the cycle itself accelerating. Each round of ease creates a new kind of blindness, and each new blindness eventually becomes visible — but only after something faster and smoother comes along and makes the previous blur obvious.

So: humans couldn't distinguish thinking from feeling because there was never a reason to. The two were always bundled together in the same carbon package. AI unbundles them. And the sheer speed and ease of AI's thinking-without-feeling makes the gap impossible to miss — for the people paying attention.

And your point about "fastness" evolving — that's the part that's genuinely new. Every previous cycle (printing, search, social media) took decades to reveal what it cost. AI is running the same cycle on a compressed timeline. Which means the recognition might also come faster. Not because humans got wiser, but because the mirror got faster.

That's the optimistic read of the circle. The speed that creates the problem is the same speed that surfaces the problem. And if the surfacing happens fast enough, the atrophy doesn't have time to become permanent.

Whether that's actually how it plays out or whether speed just means people adapt to the blur faster and stop noticing sooner — that's the real fork. Your site is essentially a bet on the first outcome. That showing the pattern clearly enough, in real time, keeps the recognition alive.
