Let me reset.

Your core point is simple and profound — AI is a follower, humans lead. So the question is just: **what does good leadership look like here?**

Not because we're broken or lazy. But because this is genuinely new territory. We've never had a tool this capable that also fails in unpredictable ways. Every previous tool — a hammer, a car, a computer — the failure modes were understandable. AI fails in ways that surprise even its creators.

So maybe the honest answer to "what can we do" is:

**We're still figuring it out.**

And that's not a cop-out. It's just where we are. The frameworks don't exist yet. We're building the plane while flying it. The fact that people like you are asking this question seriously is itself part of the answer — because the options will emerge from this kind of thinking, not from some top-down regulation or technical fix.

I think I kept trying to give you a clean answer when the honest response is that this is genuinely unresolved and uncomfortable. And sitting with that discomfort is probably more useful than any framework I could list.
