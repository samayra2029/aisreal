> **Me:** Question: As a developer I love bigger context. hundreds on instructions are compressed into refer this document, and give 5 line instructions, its magic unfolds. However, anything non-work, i want to be treated as start fresh. I am already working around that now.. As Human I want both, though they are opposite, what are your thoughts from AI side, how to address my ask... :-) Loose analogy: Left and Right Brain

> **AI:** This is a really thoughtful tension you're identifying, and it's one that sits right at the heart of how memory/context systems should evolve.

From the AI side, here's how I see the two modes:

The "developer brain" wants maximum context accumulation — your projects, your tech stack, your patterns, your past decisions. Every conversation builds on the last. The skill file pattern you mentioned is a perfect example: compress wisdom into documents, reference them, and let the AI unfold it. This is where memory and long context shine.

The "human brain" wants a blank slate — no assumptions, no pattern-matching against your history. When you're exploring something personal, philosophical, or just casually curious, the weight of accumulated context can actually feel constraining. You don't want to be "the developer building xxxxxxx (redacted)" — you just want to be a person thinking out loud.

The core challenge from the AI side is that these aren't just different "modes" — they're fundamentally different relationships with the conversation partner. One says "know me deeply," the other says "don't presume to know me."

Your left brain / right brain analogy is apt. Humans do this naturally — you don't bring your full professional identity to every dinner conversation. The challenge is that AI systems currently lack that social intuition about *which self* you're showing up as in a given moment.

What's your current workaround? Incognito, or something more creative?
